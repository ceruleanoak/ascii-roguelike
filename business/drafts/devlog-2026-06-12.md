# Pure Rogue — Boulders That Bite Back, Fire, Lightning, and 998 Fewer Lines

*A weird-busy week: a real Red-zone puzzle, a pile of elemental systems, and a refactor that finally got main.js to stop bullying me.*

The thing I actually want you to go push around is the deflector boulders in the Red zone. There are now four-orientation deflector rocks, and you can hit a boulder with the hammer to deflect it — or charge the hammer to *empower* the hit and send it somewhere meaner. It's the first piece of the Red puzzle (the "React" path) that plays like a puzzle instead of reading like a design doc. Boulders route off the deflectors, off projectiles, off each other. I spent an embarrassing amount of time just spawning boulders into deflectors to watch them ping around, which is usually a good sign.

Then I went a little wide. Fire and electricity are now their own systems instead of special-cased nonsense bolted onto combat — same for a sandstorm pass and lightning strikes in the Yellow zone, plus a Bat system and a boomerang that actually arcs back. Yellow's rivers also finally span end-to-end and carry continuous drift, so they read as rivers instead of blue rectangles that forgot to finish. None of these are *done* done, but they're real systems now, which means they compose instead of fighting each other.

My favorite small thing: when something dies, the corpse-junk it leaves behind now inherits half the velocity of the killing blow. Whack a skeleton hard enough and its detritus skids across the room. Slimes are special — instead of debris they burst into goo blobs, because of course they do. It's the kind of detail nobody asks for and everybody feels.

There's also a Fisherman NPC with actual dialogue now (new DialogueSystem), a proper pause menu, and weapon parity between the REST hub and EXPLORE so attacking works the same in both. And under all of it, a not-small refactor: I clawed 998 lines out of main.js (6475 → 6172) and finally killed the double-weapon-tick bug that had every weapon secretly tuned against an accidental double-speed clock. That one's been quietly wrong for a long time.

Still rough: the elemental systems went in fast and the tuning is vibes-based right now — fire spread and lightning timing both need a real balance pass. And telemetry is keeping me honest. The death ledger this week is mostly my own test runs, but I'll note that a *weak* Risen in the Gray zone ended four of them, three at depth 1. The terminus zone is supposed to be where you go to die, so I suppose it's working. Next up: make the Red boulder puzzle worth solving, not just fun to nudge.
